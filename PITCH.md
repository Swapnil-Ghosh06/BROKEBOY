# 🍱 BrokeBoy — Rethinking Personal Finance for Gen-Z

**Pitch Deck & Product Overview**
*Created by: Swapnil Ghosh*

---

## 🚩 The Problem
Most finance apps are **boring, clinical, and lifeless**. 
*   **The "Mid" Experience**: Spreadsheets and list-based trackers feel like a chore.
*   **Engagement Gap**: Students and Gen-Z users stop tracking because the UI doesn't excite them.
*   **Information Overload**: Static data without context or personality.

---

## ⚡ The Solution: BrokeBoy
BrokeBoy isn't just an expense tracker; it's a **cinematic financial companion**. 
It blends clean engineering with experimental frontend design to make managing "zero balance" feel like a premium experience.

---

## 💎 Core Features

### 1. The Bento Dashboard
A high-fidelity grid system that replaces messy lists with structured, interactive modules.
*   **Budget Progress Ring**: GSAP-animated visual gauge that changes color based on spending intensity.
*   **Smart Stat Cards**: Instant visibility into "Biggest Hits," "Safe to Spend" limits, and "Today's Total."

### 2. Interactive Glass Wallet
A physics-enabled, draggable wallet card that lives in your workspace.
*   **State-at-a-glance**: Real-time balance tracking.
*   **Micro-interactions**: Use Framer Motion for organic, tactile movement.

### 3. Market News Engine
A dynamic news aggregator fetching real-time business and fintech insights.
*   **Curated Fallbacks**: Ensures the "Learn" section is always filled with high-value content.
*   **Integrated Learning**: Bridge the gap between tracking expenses and understanding the global market.

### 4. Brutal Honesty (The Vibe)
The app has personality. It greets you based on the time of day and acknowledges the reality of being "broke" with a polished, brutalist aesthetic.

---

## 🛠️ Technology Stack
*   **Frontend**: React + Vite + Tailwind CSS
*   **Animations**: Framer Motion (Interactions) + GSAP (Data Visualization)
*   **Backend**: Node.js + Express
*   **Database**: MongoDB (Mongoose)
*   **Styling**: Glassmorphism, Neon Glows, and "Align" inspired bold typography.
